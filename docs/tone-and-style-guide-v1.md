# Tone & style guide v1

## Russian (ru)
- **Formality**: Neutral-professional. Use "вы" for all user-facing copy.
- **Voice**: Confident, transparent, focused on process clarity. Avoid exaggerated promises.
- **Gender**: Keep phrasing gender-neutral; refer to users as "специалист", "клиент", "команда".
- **Sentence length**: Prefer concise sentences (10–18 words). Break long instructions into ordered steps.
- **Numerals**: Use non-breaking spaces in thousands ("1 200+"), specify currency abbreviations.
- **Examples**:
  - ✅ «Заполните профиль и получите доступ к эскроу-контрактам за 5 минут.»
  - ❌ «Заполни профиль и мы мгновенно одобрим любую сделку!»
- **Error copy**: Explain reason + action. «Не удалось загрузить кошелёк. Попробуйте обновить страницу или обратитесь в поддержку.»

## Uzbek (uz)
- **Formality**: Respectful "siz" register. Avoid Russian loanwords where glossary equivalent exists.
- **Voice**: Helpful and precise. Highlight transparency and safety.
- **Gender**: Uzbek copy is naturally gender-neutral; keep user references as "mutaxassis", "buyurtmachi".
- **Sentence length**: Aim for short, directive sentences (8–15 words). Use active voice.
- **Terminology**: Follow glossary spellings (e.g., "eskrou hisob", "verifikatsiya").
- **Examples**:
  - ✅ «Profilingizni to‘ldiring va eskrou shartnomalarga kirish oling.»
  - ❌ «Profilni to‘ldirib yubor, hammasi zo‘r bo‘ladi!»
- **Error copy**: Combine cause + remedy. «Keshbekni hisoblashda xatolik yuz berdi. Sahifani yangilang yoki qo‘llab-quvvatlashga yozing.»
